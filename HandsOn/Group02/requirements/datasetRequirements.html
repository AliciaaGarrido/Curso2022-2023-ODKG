Dataset Requirements
Simple web page displaying the dataset requirements.

R1. Data are in one of the selected smart city domains.

A definition of a smart city could be, for example, a city that uses information and communication technology (ICT) to improve operational efficiency, share information with the public and provide a better quality of government service and citizen welfare. (Source)

The chosen databases record all traffic accidents that have happened inside Madrid, that are registered by a Local Police officer and that involved at least a bycicle. It specifies location, date, hour, district, type of accident and even other metrics that could be related such as weather or age of the people involved.

It can be asserted that the data is useful for both the citizens, the Traffic direction (DGT in Spain), and the emergency services. For the citizens, the data can be analyzed to determine the safety of an area to cycle in, and for pedestrians to exercise caution in some areas. For the traffic direction, it can be used to analyze where to place some preventive meassures. For the emergency services, it can be used to lessen the severity of some accidents with more localized efforts, increasing efficiency.

R3. Data have an open license so they can be published.

The data follows the legal advice found at Madrid Datos Abiertos

It asserts that the data can be reused, for both comertial and non-comertial use cases. Moreover, it concedes the Intellectual rights to use and share the data to anyone, with no exclusivity.

R4. Data can be easily linked with generic real-world entities (e.g., locations).

The data reflects occurences that happened in real geographical places. It can be linked to real-world places and entities.

Some tags in the dataset also reflect this fact, like "localizacion" and "distrito".
